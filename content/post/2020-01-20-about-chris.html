What is a Finding?

My professional career has been spent in healthcare, and most of that for the past 20 years was focused on healthcare auditing, oversight, and monitoring. In this space, the term finding is commonplace. Doctors use it to describe the results or impressions of testing, treatment, or evaluation. Auditors use the term to describe the actual state of some item that was tested against a defined standard. Findings represent what was discovered and, when necessary, are usually accompanied by recommendations for improvement. A “finding” may be that an item met standard, or a process is in compliance with policy, or a patient has no abnormalities identified. A finding is simply a recorded observation from an individual qualified to renter an opinion.
